    Score logic

    The model is visible enough to debate, not decorative enough to hide behind.

    The logic stays compact: fit decides who, timing decides when, and confidence decides whether the score deserves trust.

    The interface is organized around the three decisions that matter in RevOps: target, timing, and trust.

    • Fit favors B2C complexity, legacy pain, and commerce context.
    • Timing stacks decision-stage intent, hiring, and first-party engagement.
    • Confidence protects Salesforce from bad writes.

    When the score should be distrusted

    Conflict, stale verification, or missing fields should change routing immediately.

    Architecture

    Salesforce is the operating system. The rest of the stack exists to enrich, explain, and activate.

    The core architectural point is simple: SFDC owns the score, Marketo reads it, and governance sits before either one.
