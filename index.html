Why it scored this way
    AE rationale

    Tier A: Harbor & Pine is the kind of B2C account Gladly should pursue immediately because fit is strong, timing is live, and the confidence layer supports routing rather than undermining it.

    Tuning controls

    Fit weights

    These start on your blueprint defaults and can be adjusted live without changing the scenario data itself.

    Timing weights

    Timing saturates at 50 in the model even though the visible starter weights sum above that. The cap keeps the layer directional instead of overwhelming fit.

    Confidence and hard rules

    Use these to show why trust and architecture matter. A hot score with a bad write path should not move into outbound motion.

    Score logic

    The model is visible enough to debate, not decorative enough to hide behind.

    The logic stays compact: fit decides who, timing decides when, and confidence decides whether the score deserves trust.

    The interface is organized around the three decisions that matter in RevOps: target, timing, and trust.

    • Fit favors B2C complexity, legacy pain, and commerce context.
    • Timing stacks decision-stage intent, hiring, and first-party engagement.
    • Confidence protects Salesforce from bad writes.

    Architecture

    Salesforce is the operating system. The rest of the stack exists to enrich, explain, and activate.

    The core architectural point is simple: SFDC owns the score, Marketo reads it, and governance sits before either one.
